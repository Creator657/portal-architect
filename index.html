How to Use Portal Architect

1. Set your Mother Portal

Enter your current Overworld X and Z, pick a Grid Radius (how many portals out in each direction), then click Generate Grid. This locks in your coordinates and builds the network.

2. Reading portal names

The Mother Portal is always named M. Every other portal is named by direction and distance from it, like N3E2 (3 north, 2 east) or S1W4 (1 south, 4 west).

3. Search & Nearest tools

Portal names are case-sensitive. Searching n3e2 will not find N3E2 — match the capitalization exactly, including the "M" for Mother Portal. If you've given a portal a custom name, that match is case-sensitive too.

4. Distance & Slot Finder

Distance Calculator works between any two Overworld points, no grid needed. Slot Finder tells you the nearest portal position for any coordinate, even one you haven't generated yet.

5. Selecting a portal

Click any portal card to select it (gold outline). With one selected, use the floating buttons in the bottom-right corner: ✏️ to give it a custom name, 📋 to copy its coordinates.

6. Themes & phone view

Use 🖌️ to switch between Nether, Overworld, and Deep Dark themes. The toggle near the top switches to a phone-optimized layout.

Starting over

Click Edit Coordinates on the Mother Portal card to unlock it and generate a fresh grid. This clears the current grid, any custom names, and your selection.
